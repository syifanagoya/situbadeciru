American rapper Ice Cube, real name O’Shea Jackson, is also an activist, actor, and filmmaker. On June 15, 1969, he was born in Los Angeles, California, in the United States. The following details regarding Ice Cube’s life, including his height, weight, and other statistics:

Ice Cube Biography

In the late 1980s, Ice Cube became well-known as a member of the rap group N.W.A (Niggaz Wit Attitudes). His political and violent lyrics, which focused on social concerns impacting the African-American community, made him well-known. In 1989, Ice Cube broke away from the group and started a lucrative solo career.

Ice Cube’s solo albums, such as “AmeriKKKa’s Most Wanted” (1990), “Death Certificate” (1991), and “The Predator” (1992), received positive reviews. He frequently addressed issues like racism, police brutality, and urban life in his music. He is well known for being a gangsta rap pioneer.

Ice Cube has established a name for himself in the film sector in addition to his music career. Boyz n the Hood (1991), Friday (1995), Barbershop (2002), and the N.W.A. biopic “Straight Outta Compton” (2015) are just a few of the films he has been in or produced. He has gained notoriety in Hollywood because to the positive reviews his acting performances have earned.

Ice Cube Height and Weight

The estimated height and weight of Ice Cube are 5 feet 8 inches (173 cm) and 170 pounds (77 kg), respectively. Please be aware that these metrics are merely estimates and could change.

Ice Cube Children

O’Shea Jackson Jr., Darrell Jackson, Karima Jackson, and Shareef Jackson are the names of Ice Cube’s four children.

By leveraging his position to bring attention to social and political issues, Ice Cube has had a lasting impression on the music and film industries. His contributions have solidified his standing as a cultural icon and gained him a devoted following.
