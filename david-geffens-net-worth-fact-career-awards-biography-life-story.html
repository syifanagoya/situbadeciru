David Geffen is an American business magnate, producer, film studio executive, and philanthropist. Geffen is best recognized as the co-founder of “Asylum Records” and “DreamWorks SKG”. With a net worth of $8 billion, Geffen is one of the richest people in the entertainment industry. In 2007, Geffen was ranked first in the list of 50 “Most Powerful Gay Men and Women in America”.

What is David Geffen Famous for?

  • Famous as billionaire business tycoon and co-founder of “Asylum Records”. 

Where was David Geffen Born?

David Geffen was born on February 21, 1943, in New York City, United States. His birth name is David Lawrence Geffen. His nationality is American. Geffen belongs to Mixed ethnicity and is of Polish-Jewish and Ukrainian-Jewish descent. Pisces is his zodiac sign. 

Geffen is the youngest son born to Abraham Geffen(father) and Batya Volovskaya(mother). His father died when he was 16, while her mother had a clothing store in Borough Park, “Chic Corsets by Geffen”. His parents were Jewish immigrants. David grew up alongside his older brother, Mitchell Geffen. 

Geffen graduated from Brooklyn’s New Utrecht High School in 1960 with around 66%. Later, he attended the University of Texas at Austin for a semester, and then he got transferred to Brooklyn College before he dropped out. 

Later, he moved to Los Angeles, California to find a job in the entertainment business. He also attended Santa Monica College in Santa Monica, California. 

David Geffen’s Career Highlights

  • Geffen began his entertainment career in the mailroom at the William Morris Agency (WMA) as a talent agent. He also had a brief appearance in the 1961 film, “The Explosive Generation”. 
  • Soon then, Geffen left William Morris to become a personnel manager. 
  • In 1971, Geffen co-founded “Asylum Records” with Elliot Roberts which later became a generator of the Southern California folk-rock sound. However, he left the Asylum. 
  • Later in 1975, he worked as a vice-chairman of “Warner Bros.” film studios. However, he retired soon with a false diagnosis of him catching cancer which later was corrected giving a clean bill of health to Geffen. 
  • In 1980, he recruited Warner Bros. Records as president and also founded Geffen Records. His records’ first artist to be signed was Donna Summer. 
  • His labels’ breakout point came with the release of John Lennon’s album, “Double Fantasy” in November 1980. He continued to run the label before leaving “Geffen Records” in 1995.
  • In 1982, he founded a film production company, “Geffen Film Company” which produced several comedy films including Little Shop of Horrors, Risky Business and Beetlejuice. 
  • Later in 1994, Geffen co-founded another film label, “DreamWorks SKG” studio with Steven Spielberg and Jeffrey Katzenberg. He remained until 2008. 
  • Besides, he was an early financial supporter of the then-President Bill Clinton and also of Barack Obama. 
  • Geffen won the “Ahmet Ertegun Award” from the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2010. 
  • Later in February 2011, he was awarded the President’s Merit Award at the 53rd Grammy Awards.
