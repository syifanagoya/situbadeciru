Bill Burgess Obituary: Remembering a Legendary Football Coach

It is with great sadness that we announce the passing of Bill Burgess, a legendary football coach known for his tenure at Jacksonville State University. At the age of 82, Burgess leaves behind a remarkable legacy that will forever be remembered in the world of football. His impact on the sport was so profound that in 2010, the field at JSU Stadium was renamed in his honor, a testament to his extraordinary contributions to the game.

Accomplishments at Jacksonville State

During his tenure at Jacksonville State University, Bill Burgess achieved remarkable success, solidifying his status as one of the most successful football coaches in the school’s history. With an impressive record of 84-49-4, Burgess led the Gamecocks to four Gulf South Conference championships, showcasing the team’s talent and his exceptional coaching abilities. However, his crowning achievement came in 1992 when he guided the team to victory in the Division II championship, bringing immense pride to the university and establishing Jacksonville State as a force to be reckoned with in college football.
